Organizational Development

Organizations need qualities of a living organism to be useful and make a profit in an ever-changing world. We can start with doing a complete health check: are the organization’s feedback loops closed, is it well-connected to customers needs? Then, not to waste resources, are repetitive processes clearly defined, streamlined and as automated as reasonable? If we need AI, I’m working with appliedAI, a TUM gGmbH. Only when the basics are solid we go on to optionality: does the organization have a stringent and continuous system to develop business opportunities? We’re building those steps on tools already used in your organization, supplemented with modules to fill possible gaps. To add a broad background to my experience I’m doing my projects in sparring or cooperation with berylls or EquWin. Even if we’re starting small (for example establishing a system that self-enforces clear responsibilities) I’ll put a focus on the inherent design principles of your organization. Only by putting them first the whole system will self-improve even further in the long run.

Visualization

All organizational success is built on previous learning. True learning needs on a clear connection between action and effect, the feedback delay being critical for learning effectiveness. In many processes those connections are not explicit or hampered by manual data collection. Selecting the right results and indicators for your core processes; auto­mating data parsing and visualization add motivation and set your organization up for self-improvement. Apart from that you’re saving manual work in generation of repetitive reports of course.

  • Show more

    Another aspect I like to use visualizations for is providing the big picture, the organization’s health to each and every employee interested. In times of high volatility and implicitly more decisions, the context of everyone’s own decisions gets more important to know. When creating data visualizations I work directly with the prospect users, integrate them in a design thinking process to evaluate the visualizations’ effect as early as possible. The visualizations are as minimalistic as possible, only showing the most crucial metrics, allowing to dig deeper only if necessary.

    Tools I use:

    • JavaScript and d3.js to build visualizations that run in every web browser
    • RawGraphs or Tableau to build prototypes
    • Python for data cleaning
